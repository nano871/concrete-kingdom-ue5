# Knowledge Card: GTA V Traffic AI & Pedestrian Schedule System

## Summary

GTA V's traffic AI and pedestrian system form the backbone of Los Santos' "lived-in" feeling. Built on the RAGE engine with Euphoria physics, the system manages thousands of simulated agents across a 75 sq km world using aggressive LOD streaming, population density budgets, and zone-specific behavior patterns.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Lane-Based Traffic on Spline Road Networks
GTA V's traffic AI does not use generic navmesh pathfinding for cars. Vehicles follow **spline-defined road lanes** baked into the world geometry. Each road segment contains:
- Lane centers as path splines
- Speed limits (per road type)
- Intersection data (stop signs, traffic light timing)
- Lane change merge points

*Design implication:* Cars travel predictably along road geometry. Chaos emerges when the player disrupts these paths (blocking lanes, crashing, shooting). The system is cheap because it doesn't recalculate paths — it just follows splines with simple obstacle avoidance.

### 2. Population Density Budget & Zone-Based Spawning
The game maintains a **pool of active NPCs** (vehicles + pedestrians) with hard caps determined by the platform:
- **PS3/Xbox 360:** ~25 active NPCs visible at once
- **PS4/Xbox One:** ~75 active NPCs  
- **PC (max settings):** 100+ active NPCs (population density slider 0–100%)

Spawning is **zone-driven**, not player-position-driven alone:
- Downtown Los Santos: high density (cars + pedestrians)
- Suburbs: medium density (more pedestrians, fewer cars)
- Vinewood/Beverly Hills: luxury vehicle bias
- Countryside/rural: very sparse (wildlife instead)
- Industrial: truck/heavy vehicle bias

The game pre-caches pedestrian models for the current zone to avoid streaming stutter. Model variety per zone is prioritized over total unique models.

### 3. Pedestrian Schedules & Behavior Sets (Pre-RDR2 Limited)
GTA V's pedestrian schedules are **simpler than RDR2's full daily routine system**, but still effective:
- Pedestrians have **behavior states** rather than full schedules:
  - Walking (random destination within block)
  - Standing/Talking (scripted loops, cell phone, leaning against walls)
  - Waiting at crosswalks
  - Entering/exiting vehicles (pre-scripted animations)
  - Running (panic state, triggered by nearby chaos)
- Behavior state transitions are **timer-based with random variance**
- Different neighborhoods get different behavior subsets:
  - Beach area: pedestrians in swimwear, jogging, rollerblading
  - Business district: pedestrians in suits, on phones, hailing taxis

**Euphoria reactivity:** When the player hits a pedestrian with a car, Euphoria's physics engine takes over — the body reacts procedurally to impact force, direction, and surface. This sells the "real" feel more than any animation system could.

### 4. Wanted System Integration with Traffic/Pedestrian AI
The police wanted level is deeply integrated:
- **Wanted Level 1:** Police spawn at nearest patrol point; they use same road splines but with higher speed and intercept pathfinding toward player's last known position
- **Wanted Level 2:** Police set up roadblocks (dynamically placed vehicles that despawn when player leaves area)
- **Wanted Level 3+:** Police helicopters use separate AI — they track player with spotlight, share position data to ground units
- **Pedestrian reactions:** Civilians flee, cower, or film with phones; some call 911 (triggers police response even at wanted level 0)
- **Search mode (broken line-of-sight):** Police fan out using the road network; "last seen" mechanic uses NavMesh zones rather than exact position

The **911 call system** is the underappreciated bridge: NPC witnesses have a "crime severity" threshold. Minor fender-benders don't trigger calls. Gunfire + witnesses = automatic wanted level in populated areas. This creates natural gameplay pressure to commit crimes in isolated locations.

### 5. Traffic Light & Intersection System
Traffic lights operate on **synchronized timers** per intersection cluster:
- Lights cycle through green/yellow/red on fixed schedules
- Timer schedules vary between intersections to create "wave" patterns (like real cities)
- Damage model: shooting a traffic light breaks it (stuck on one color or dead)
- Cars run red lights during chaos (panic state overrides rules)
- In police chase mode, traffic AI biases toward running lights to create player obstacles

*Key insight for Concrete Kingdom:* The traffic wave system alone makes driving feel dynamic. Players naturally learn which intersections are green on approach and which force stops — this spatial learning is a form of environmental mastery.

## Application to Concrete Kingdom

### Immediate Implementation Recommendations

1. **Spline-Based Road Network, Not NavMesh**
   - Build roads as spline paths with lane data baked into the world partition
   - Each road segment carries: speed limit, lane count, intersection type, allowed vehicle types
   - Avoid generic navmesh pathfinding for cars — it's expensive and produces jerky movement
   - **Prototype:** Create a 3-intersection graybox loop with 10 AI cars following splines. Measure CPU cost vs navmesh approach.

2. **Zone-Based Population Budgets**
   - Define population density per district (downtown = 100%, industrial = 40%, rural = 10%)
   - Vehicle type bias per zone (luxury cars in financial district, trucks in industrial)
   - Pedestrian model pools pre-cached per zone
   - Hard cap on total active NPCs based on target platform (Concrete Kingdom target: ~80 NPCs on consoles, 120+ on PC)

3. **Traffic Light Synchronization for Emergent Feel**
   - Implement intersection controllers with configurable timer patterns
   - Each district gets different timing profiles (downtown: long cycles, suburbs: short)
   - Allow damage/deactivation — players notice and exploit broken lights
   - **Prototype:** 2 intersections with different timing patterns, measure if players notice the wave effect

4. **Pedestrian Behavior State Machine**
   - Keep it simple: 5–7 behavior states with random transitions
   - Zone-specific state pools (beach: more joggers, commercial: more shoppers)
   - Euphoria-style physics reaction to vehicle impact (UE5 Chaos Physics or custom spring-rig)
   - **Event-driven panic:** Nearby explosion/gunfire triggers mass flee state

5. **Witness/Crime Reporting System**
   - Pedestrians have "witness range" and "crime severity threshold"
   - Minor accidents ignored; gunfire + witness = call for response
   - Response time varies by zone (downtown: faster, rural: slower)
   - Creates natural risk/reward for location choice in mission design

### What NOT to Do (Lessons from GTA V Limitations)
- **Don't** give every pedestrian a full 24-hour schedule — RDR2 proved this is extremely expensive. Use behavioral states + zone pools instead.
- **Don't** pathfind every vehicle individually — use spline following for 90% of traffic, reserve full pathfinding for police intercepts and mission-critical vehicles.
- **Don't** make pedestrians too smart — they should react to immediate stimuli, not simulate long-term goals. The illusion of intelligence costs much less than actual intelligence.
